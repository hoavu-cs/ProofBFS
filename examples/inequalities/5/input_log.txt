\[

\]
Equality holds if and only if $ab=c^2$ and $\frac{a+b}{c}=1+\sqrt{3}$.

proof:

\[
(a+b-c)\left(\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}-\frac{1}{c}\right)=c(p-1)\cdot\frac{q-1}{c}=(p-1)(q-1)=3.
\]
Since the left‑hand side is positive, both factors $p-1$ and $q-1$ are positive; hence $p>1$ and $q>1$.
Applying the AM–GM inequality to the positive numbers $p-1$ and $q-1$ yields
\[
(p-1)+(q-1)\ge 2\sqrt{(p-1)(q-1)}=2\sqrt{3},
\]
so that $p+q\ge 2+2\sqrt{3}$, which is exactly the desired inequality.
Equality in AM–GM occurs when $p-1=q-1$, i.e. $p=q$, and then $(p-1)^2=3$ gives $p=q=1+\sqrt{3}$.
The condition $p=q$ is equivalent to $\frac{a+b}{c}=c\left(\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}\right)$, which simplifies to $ab=c^2$.  Thus equality holds precisely when $ab=c^2$ and $\frac{a+b}{c}=1+\sqrt{3}$.
